  5. Real-World Application
    ⚙️ Civil Engineering — Survey Problem

    A survey team needs the straight-line distance between two GPS markers on opposite banks of a river. They cannot walk directly between them. Using GPS coordinates (in units of 100 meters), they plot Station A at (1, 2) and Station B at (7, 10) on a coordinate grid. From there, they walk the right-angle legs and apply the formula to compute the uncrossable span.

    Find the direct distance between Station A and Station B. Express your answer in grid units, then interpret what that means in meters.

    Dashed orange = the span they can't walk. Teal dashes = the legs they can measure on land.

    grid units

    Since each unit = 100 meters, the river span is 1,000 m (1 km). This is exactly how field surveyors compute uncrossable distances — GPS data gives the coordinates, the distance formula gives the span.
